# ICC Trading Strategy Guide
## Trade by Sci's Indication, Correction, Continuation Framework

### What is ICC?

ICC is a **price action trading strategy** developed by Trade by Sci that identifies high-probability setups through three distinct market phases:

1. **INDICATION** - A structure break that indicates potential trend direction
2. **CORRECTION** - A pullback that retests the broken level
3. **CONTINUATION** - Confirmation of the trend, providing the entry signal

### The Three Phases Explained

#### Phase 1: INDICATION
**What it is:** Price breaks a key support or resistance level, forming a new high or new low.

**What it signals:**
- Breaking **ABOVE resistance** = Bullish indication (look for LONG setups)
- Breaking **BELOW support** = Bearish indication (look for SHORT setups)

**Key Rules:**
- ❌ **DO NOT** enter on the initial break
- ✅ Mark the broken level - it becomes important for the next phase
- The break must be a clear move, not just a wick/spike

**Example:**
```
Price has been respecting $100 resistance → breaks to $102
This is your INDICATION that price may be turning bullish
```

#### Phase 2: CORRECTION
**What it is:** Price pulls back to retest the previously broken level.

**What it signals:**
- The market is "grabbing liquidity" at the broken level
- Smart money is accumulating positions
- The level is being tested as new support (former resistance) or new resistance (former support)

**Key Rules:**
- ❌ **DO NOT** enter during the correction itself
- ✅ Wait for price to reach the broken level (within ~0.5-1% tolerance)
- ✅ Watch for rejection or acceptance at the level
- Price should not completely fail the retest (breaking back through in the opposite direction)

**Example:**
```
After breaking $100 resistance → price pulls back to $100.50
This is your CORRECTION - price is retesting the broken level
```

#### Phase 3: CONTINUATION
**What it is:** Price resumes moving in the indicated direction after successfully retesting.

**What it signals:**
- The retest held - the level is confirmed as new support/resistance
- Momentum is resuming in the original direction
- **THIS IS YOUR ENTRY SIGNAL**

**Entry Rules:**
- ✅ Enter LONG when: Bearish indication → Correction retest → New higher high
- ✅ Enter SHORT when: Bullish indication → Correction retest → New lower low
- ✅ Confirm with momentum (price moving decisively away from retest level)
- Stop loss: Just beyond the correction level (invalidation point)

**Example:**
```
After retesting $100.50 → price moves to $103
This is your CONTINUATION - enter LONG position
Stop loss: Below $100 (below the retested level)
```

### Complete ICC Trade Example

**Bullish ICC Setup:**
```
1. INDICATION: Price breaks resistance at $100, reaching $102
   → Mark $100 as key level, set alert for pullback

2. CORRECTION: Price pulls back to $100.50 (retest zone)
   → Wait for reaction, don't enter yet

3. CONTINUATION: Price bounces from $100.50, breaks $102, reaches $104
   → ENTER LONG at $102 break or $104 confirmation
   → Stop Loss: $99.50 (below retest level)
   → Target: $108+ (3-5% or next resistance)
```

**Bearish ICC Setup:**
```
1. INDICATION: Price breaks support at $100, drops to $98
   → Mark $100 as key level, set alert for pullback

2. CORRECTION: Price rallies back to $99.50 (retest zone)
   → Wait for rejection, don't enter yet

3. CONTINUATION: Price rejects from $99.50, breaks $98, drops to $96
   → ENTER SHORT at $98 break or $96 confirmation
   → Stop Loss: $100.50 (above retest level)
   → Target: $92- (3-5% or next support)
```

### Timeframe Strategy

**Multi-Timeframe Analysis:**
- **Higher Timeframe (1H, 4H, Daily):** Identify key support/resistance levels
- **Lower Timeframe (5m, 15m):** Find precise entry on continuation phase
- **Confirmation:** Both timeframes should align

**Why Multi-Timeframe?**
- Higher timeframes give you the "big picture" structure
- Lower timeframes give you precise, low-risk entries
- Avoids getting faked out by noise

### Key Support/Resistance Identification

**What Makes a Level "Key":**
1. **Multiple touches** - Price has respected it 2+ times
2. **Round numbers** - $100, $50, $1000 (psychological levels)
3. **Recent swing highs/lows** - Obvious pivot points
4. **Volume confirmation** - High volume at the level
5. **Clean breaks** - Level acts as clear support/resistance

**How to Find Them:**
- Look for obvious horizontal levels where price has reversed multiple times
- Identify swing highs (peaks) and swing lows (valleys)
- Mark levels where price has consolidated or bounced strongly

### Risk Management

**Position Sizing:**
- Risk 1-2% of account per trade maximum
- Calculate based on stop loss distance

**Stop Loss Placement:**
- For LONG: Below the correction retest level (with buffer)
- For SHORT: Above the correction retest level (with buffer)
- Buffer: 0.5-1% beyond the level to avoid stop hunting

**Profit Targets:**
- Minimum: 2:1 reward-to-risk ratio
- Primary Target: Next major support/resistance level
- Swing Target: 3-5% move (much larger than scalping)
- Trail stop once in profit to lock in gains

### Common Mistakes to Avoid

❌ **Entering on the Indication** - This is the break, not the entry! You'll get faked out.

❌ **Entering during the Correction** - Price might continue falling, you haven't confirmed the setup yet.

❌ **Ignoring the Correction Phase** - If there's no retest, there's no ICC setup. Wait for the next one.

❌ **Trading Every Break** - Not every support/resistance break will lead to an ICC setup. Be selective.

❌ **Using Too-Tight Stops** - Give the retest room to breathe. Stops should be beyond the invalidation level.

❌ **No Defined Levels** - You MUST have pre-marked support/resistance levels to trade ICC properly.

### Psychology and Discipline

**Patience is Key:**
- ICC requires waiting for all three phases to complete
- You will miss trades if you jump in early
- Better to miss a trade than to take a bad entry

**Trust the Process:**
- Not every indication will lead to a full ICC setup
- Some corrections will fail (and that's okay - no entry = no loss)
- Only trade when all three phases align

**Journaling:**
- Mark every ICC setup (even if you don't trade it)
- Note which phases completed/failed
- Review to improve level identification
